Ponziani Opening

1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. c3 ...

The Ponziani Opening is a chess opening that begins with the moves: 1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. c3

3 ... Bc5

The move Bc5 is a mistake because it runs directly into 4. d4, making the black player into trading 4 ... exd4 5 cxd4 and giving a helpful check on 5. ... Bb4+ 6. Nc3 or retreating back to Bb6, a waste of tempo.

3 ... Nf6

The most logical move after 3. c3 is 3 ... Nf6, attacking our pawn on e4. But we shock our opponent by continuing with the move 4. d4.

If our opponent continues with a passive move such as 4. ... d6, we can simply continue to develop with Bd3 and O-O. However, let's see what happens if black takes the seemingly free pawn on e4

The game gets tricky after d5 followed by Nxe5, most online games ends with 6. ... d6, losing immediately to Bb5+. However if our opponent sees through our trap and follows up with 6. ... Ng6 7. Qe2, the game continues with play from both side.
